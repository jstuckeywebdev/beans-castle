Bean's Castle - Gameplay Instructions

Bean's Castle is a solo dungeon crawler designed to be played using a standard chess set, a deck of playing cards, two six-sided dice (2d6), and pen & paper to track health (or just a chess set and this companion). The dungeon is randomly generated through dice rolls, ensuring each playthrough is unique.

  • Defeat the enemy King to win.
  • If your King dies, you lose.

  • King (Healer) – Uses the Hearts deck.
    • Heals self or adjacent allies.
    • Can revive adjacent allies with 1 HP (they can act immediately).
  • Rook (Tank) – Uses the Spades deck.
    • Draws enemy attention (agro).
    • Absorbs damage.
  • Knight (Melee DPS) – Uses the Clubs deck.
    • Deals damage to adjacent enemies.
    • Can move 2 spaces, or move 1 and attack in the same turn.
  • Bishop (Ranged DPS) – Uses the Diamonds deck.
    • Attacks at range with reduced damage by distance.
    • Targets must be in straight line of sight (orthogonal or diagonal).

  • Black King – 50 HP
    • Ranged attacks without damage penalty.
  • Black Queen – 40 HP
  • 2 Rooks – 30 HP each
  • 2 Knights – 20 HP each
    • Move 2 spaces if in direct line of sight to a party member.
  • 2 Bishops – 20 HP each
    • Ranged attacks with reduced damage by distance.

  • Separate the playing cards into 4 decks by suit. Shuffle each deck.
  • Party Start: All party members begin on tile A1. Only on turn 1 may multiple members share this tile. All but one must move from A1 in the first turn.
  • Wall (Pawn) Placement:
    • Roll a d6. Alternate between wall tiles and empty tiles using the roll result.
    • Move right to left, then down the board to place pawns.
    • Walls may not be orthogonally adjacent. Skip to next valid space if needed.
  • Enemy Placement:
    • Place the Black King on H8.
    • Roll a d6, count that many spaces from the King (including walls) and place the Queen.
    • Repeat the roll and placement process for each remaining enemy.
    • If an enemy lands on a wall, continue to the next empty tile.

Player Phase: Each party member chooses one action:

  • Move – 1 space in any direction (Knight moves 2 or can move 1 and attack).
    • Party members may pass between diagonally adjacent walls (pawns). Orthogonally adjacent walls may not be passed through.
  • Use Ability – Flip top card of that member's deck for power.
  • Hold – Take no action.

Enemy Phase:

  • Enemies are resolved in order from the bottom-left to top-right (left to right, bottom to top).
  • Targeting:
    • Enemies target the nearest party member.
    • The Rook (tank) always takes priority as a target when multiple party members are equidistant.
    • If there are multiple valid targets and one is not the rook, randomly select a party member by rolling a d6.
    • This targeting rule applies to both attacks and movement.
  • For Each Enemy (in order):
    • Attack: If possible, the enemy will attack a party member.
      • Enemies adjacent to a party member will attack.
      • Ranged enemies (Bishops and Black King) attack if they have a direct line of sight, orthogonally or diagonally.
      • Ranged enemies deal 2d6 damage minus the number of empty tiles between them and the target (except the Black King, who has no penalty).
    • Move: If the enemy did not attack:
      • If the enemy has a direct line of sight to a party member, it moves 1 space toward them (Knights move 2 spaces in this case).
      • If the enemy does not have line of sight:
        • Roll 2d6. Treat the result as a clock direction (e.g. 6 = 6 o'clock).
        • Move the enemy 1 space (Knights also move only 1 space in this case) in that direction.
        • If the target space is occupied or invalid, rotate counter-clockwise until a valid space is found.
      • Note: Enemies may pass between diagonally adjacent walls (pawns). They may not pass between orthogonally adjacent walls.
